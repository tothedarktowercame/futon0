## 3. Strike a chord — the parallel-run logic + the readiness gate

- **Parallel, not serial.** Running ≥2 instances *at once* is the point: agreement across domains IS the
  evidence that the **method** (not the domain) is what works. One domain alone = whistling in the dark;
  two-plus resonating = striking a chord.
- **Local-first / the readiness gate (Joe).** The **WM**, **mission-corpus** and **code** instances are
  LOCAL — on-laptop, fast feedback, no superpod. The **math** instance is superpod-bound (Rob). *"If we can't
  do structure-learning with the WM, we're not ready to do it on the superpod."* So the local instances are
  the **proving-ground**; they de-risk the math/superpod spend.

## 4. Joint completion criterion (CHARTER)

The standard is witnessed when **the same Bayesian-structure-learning method, instantiated independently in
≥2 LOCAL domains (WM + one of mission-corpus / code), demonstrably learns structure *and* improves its own
model** — i.e. the transfer is shown locally *before* the superpod (math) spend. The math instance is the
*beneficiary* of the proven transfer, not a precondition.
